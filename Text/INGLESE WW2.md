# Testo Integrale
## Causes
The war grew out of the harsh conditions imposed by the Treaty of Versailles (1919), which left Germany economically devastated and politically humiliated. This fueled the rise of totalitarian regimes: Hitler in Germany, Mussolini in Italy, and militarist leaders in Japan. Germany pursued aggressive expansionism — remilitarizing the Rhineland, annexing Austria (1938) and Czechoslovakia (1939) — while Western powers failed to stop it through a policy of appeasement. The invasion of Poland on September 1, 1939 finally triggered the conflict.
## Sides
**Allies:** United Kingdom, France, Soviet Union (from 1941), United States (from 1941), China.
**Axis:** Germany, Italy, Japan.
## Key Events
- **1939** — Germany invades Poland; UK and France declare war.
- **1940** — Fall of France; Battle of Britain; Germany dominates Western Europe.
- **1941** — Operation Barbarossa: Germany invades the USSR. Japan attacks Pearl Harbor (December 7); the USA enters the war.
- **1942–43** — Battle of Stalingrad: catastrophic German defeat, turning point on the Eastern Front.
- **1943** — Allied landings in Sicily and Italy; Italy surrenders and switches sides.
- **1944** — D-Day: Allied forces land in Normandy (June 6), opening a second front in Western Europe.
- **1945** — Soviet forces capture Berlin; Germany surrenders (May 8, V-E Day). Atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima (August 6) and Nagasaki (August 9); Japan surrenders (September 2, V-J Day).
## Winners and Losers
**Winners:** The Allied powers — primarily the USA, USSR, UK, and France. The United States and the Soviet Union emerged as the two dominant world superpowers.
**Losers:** Germany, Italy, and Japan were defeated, occupied, and rebuilt under Allied supervision. Germany was divided into occupation zones (East and West).
## Consequences — The Cold War
The end of World War II reshaped the entire international order:
- The **United Nations** was founded in 1945 to maintain peace and prevent future conflicts.
- Europe was effectively divided into two blocs: the **Western bloc** (capitalist democracies, led by the USA) and the **Eastern bloc** (communist states, led by the USSR).
- The **Cold War** began around 1947 — a prolonged ideological, political, and military rivalry between the two superpowers, stopping short of direct armed conflict.
- The **Marshall Plan** (1948) provided US economic aid to rebuild Western Europe and contain communism.
- **NATO** was founded in 1949 as a collective defense alliance among Western nations.
- Germany was formally split into the **Federal Republic** (West) and the **Democratic Republic** (East).
- A global **nuclear arms race** began, with both superpowers developing and stockpiling atomic weapons.
